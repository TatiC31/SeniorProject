Technology & Mental Health

People's mental health and how much they are on the internet are connected. Being addicted to technology is not healthy for people's brains or bodies at all. Being addicted could negatively affect mental health and could possibly harm their emotional state. Technology addiction can cause people severe mood swings, anxiety and even possibly develop sleep disorders. Teenagers are constantly on their phones day and night. Those that are addicted to technology tend to develop sleeping disorders because they are up all night using technology.

There are studies that have shown the brain scans of teens and young adults that suffer from internet addiction are similar to those who suffer from substance addiction (Hazelden.) “Damage to brain systems connecting emotional processing, attention, and decision-making are affected in both substance addicts and technology addicts. This discovery shows that being hooked on a tech behavior can, in some ways, be as physically damaging as an addiction to alcohol and other drug use” (Hazelden).

Nowadays whoever is suffering from hard times they often use technology as an escape from reality. Our new generation often use technology as a distraction to make themselves feel better or as an escape from whatever they are going through. There was a survey taken in 2019 that showed 40% of technology users in the U.S population are addicted to social media. (Zuckerman.)
